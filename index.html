Research Interests

My current research interests revolve around the following key questions in AI-assisted programming:

  • How can we leverage state-of-the-art machine learning models to create new programming abstractions? How can we leverage symbolic and neural techniques to synthesize programs in a correct, scalable, user-friendly way?

  • What are the right abstractions for programming tasks involving Large Language Model (LLM) invocations?

  • How can we effectively integrate programming interfaces with program synthesizers?

Notes to Prospective Students

Research Scope: My research is grounded in programming languages, formal methods, and program synthesis. I do use LLMs and machine learning extensively, but my core interests are in reasoning about software, programs, and logic — LLMs are one of several tools I use, not the primary object of study. I may be less well-suited to advise students whose primary interest is LLMs or NLP research itself.

Preferred Background (any level): I am especially interested in working with students who are clearly self-motivated and have a solid mathematical background. Prior experience with systems programming is a plus. No prior research experience is required.

Prospective MSc/PhD Students: I am recruiting 1-2 fully funded MSc/PhD students in the upcoming cycle. Please note that our MSc program is fully research based and fully funded (same as the first 2 years in a PhD program), and our PhD program is shorter and assumes prior research training. As such, we do not directly admit bachelor students into the PhD program — students typically join via the MSc first. I only take MSc students who are interested in pursuing a PhD degree. Before emailing, please take a look at a few of my recent papers and briefly mention in your email which directions interest you and why. Please email me at jocelyn.chen@ualberta.ca and include "Prospective Student" in the email subject line.
